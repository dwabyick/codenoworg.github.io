Curriculum

Basics

Go through those (in order) with the students to get them to understand the programming tools they’ll be using all throughout the CodeNow curriculum.

  • Command Prompt Basics – Get familiar with the command prompt inside Windows machines. Master creating, copying, and deleting files and folders all without using your mouse.
  • Sublime Text – Get familiar with SublimeText and customize your text editor

Beginner projects

Projects intended for students who are still beginning with Ruby.

Projects to do in order

  • Hi-Lo Game – Your first exposure to Ruby, focusing on the most important concepts and syntax elements.
  • Rock, Paper, Scissors – Try your hand at building a more complex game that builds off of the concepts learned in Hi-Lo.

Extra projects

  • Brain Refresh – Review of major topics covered by the beginner projects.
  • Calculator – Writing a basic calculator in Ruby
  • Colors! – Fun with colors in the terminal

Intermediate projects

Projects for students who have completed the Beginner level.

Projects to do in order

  • Encryptor – Write a program that enables you to encrypt and decrypt secret messages.
  • TwitterBot – Write a TwitterBot that will allow you to tweet from the command prompt! Learn how to make the bot send private messages and also spam your followers!

Extra projects

  • Hangman – Writing a hangman game

Alumni projects

Those projects are intended for alumni – students who have gone through the 3 weeks of CodeNow curriculum. They require the student to be fluent with all the Ruby basics: variables, arrays, conditionals, iteration, etc.

  • Messengr – Master interacting with the web via Ruby by building your own chat room!

Standalone Projects

  • Arduino – Do some hardware hacking with an Arduino, and learn to control LEDs from a Ruby script.
