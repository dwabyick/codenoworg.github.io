Cloud IDE

Cloud 9 is a web-based development environment. A development environment refers to the collection of applications and tools programmers have installed on their machines that helps them develop their applications, debug their code, and test their programs. Because Cloud 9 sits on the cloud you are able to work from any computer with Internet access, no need for a local workstation. In addition, it allows you to collaborate on a project in real-time.

Create a Free Cloud 9 Account

  1. In your browser, type c9.io in the address field. This should take you to the homepage of Cloud 9.
  2. Clink on the “SIGN-UP” button and fill in the necessary information to create a free account.
  3. Make sure to uncheck the “Receive our monthly newsletter” checkbox.

Create a New Workspace

  1. Click the "CREATE NEW WORKSPACE" button in your Dashboard, and choose “Create a New Workspace”.
  2. Enter a workspace name.
  3. Choose to make it Private (only accessible to you and people you share it with).
  4. Choose “Hosted” as the type of workspace you want to create. This is a regular Cloud9 workspace.
  5. Also, select 'Custom' workspace.
  6. Next press “CREATE”. You should be able to see your new workspace in the dashboard.
  7. If it seems like “processing” your workspace is taking a really long time, try signing out and signing back in.

Enabling Auto-Save

  1. Click on your workspace, you should see a button to “START EDITING” on the right hand side of the screen.
  2. Click the “START EDITING” button, this should open your workspace.
  3. Click on the “gear” icon on the top right corner. This will allow you to adjust the settings of your workspace.
  4. When you first click on the “gear” icon you should see a “Project Setting” page and on the left hand side, a menu with the “Project Settings” option highlighted.
  5. Underneath the “Project Settings” option you should see a “Settings” option.
  6. Click on the “Settings” option and select the “Save” option from underneath it. This should allow you to turn on the “Enable Auto-Save” setting.
  7. Click on the “gear” icon again to close the settings.
